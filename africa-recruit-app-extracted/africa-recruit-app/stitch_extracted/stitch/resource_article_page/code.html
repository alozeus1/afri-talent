VISA & RELOCATION

The Complete Guide to Tech Visa Sponsorship for African Developers

Everything you need to know about navigating the visa sponsorship landscape as a developer from the continent or diaspora.

Relocating for a software engineering role is one of the most transformative steps an African developer can take in their career. However, the complexity of visa sponsorship often feels like a barrier. This guide breaks down the process into actionable steps for talent looking to move to tech hubs in Europe, North America, and beyond.

Understanding the Landscape

Not all companies are created equal when it comes to sponsorship. Large-scale tech giants (MAMAA) and Series B+ startups often have the infrastructure to handle international relocations. In countries like Germany or the UK, employers must be registered as official sponsors before they can offer you a job that qualifies for a visa.

info

Pro Tip: Verify Sponsorship Status

Always verify your potential employer's sponsorship license status before starting the interview process. In the UK, you can check the public Register of Licensed Sponsors.

Key Documents Needed

Preparing your documentation early can save weeks of processing time. Most skilled worker visas require a baseline set of credentials:

  • Proof of Professional Experience: Standardized CV and portfolio of work (GitHub/Live projects).
  • Academic Certificates: Degree certificates, often requiring official translation or equivalence verification (e.g., ZAB in Germany).
  • Language Proficiency: Standardized test results (IELTS/TOEFL) if moving to an English-speaking country.
  • Passport Validity: Ensure your travel document is valid for at least 2 years.

Navigating the Interview Process

When interviewing for a sponsored role, be transparent about your location early. Highlight your willingness to relocate and mention any specific visa categories you might be eligible for (like the EU Blue Card or the UK's Global Talent Visa).
